BILLIE HOLIDAY

Billie Holiday, born Eleanora Fagan in 1915, is considered one of the greatest jazz vocalists of all time. She was born to a turbulent family and had no formal musical training; however, by the time of her career in the 1930s, she had developed a unique voice that conveyed deep emotion and vulnerability. In her time, she collaborated with legendary musicians like Lester Young and Artie Shaw, who helped write and record classics such as "Strange Fruit" and "God Bless the Child."

Her life was marked by personal struggles, including substance abuse and legal troubles, which she often channeled into her music. Despite these challenges, Holiday's impact on jazz and popular music remains profound. She won five Grammy Awards, was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, and continues to be celebrated as one of the most influential singers in jazz history.
